Presenting the AI Moral Code at ICAD 2025

Author: Ran Hinrichs

Date: May 2025

At ICAD 2025, I will present Advancing Ethical AI: A Methodological and Empirical Approach to the AI Moral Code, a paper that formalizes the NRBC architecture as an ethical governance model for AI. The research draws from 291 AI ethics documents and projects values into a layered framework—Normative, Regulatory, Behavioral, and Conceptual—that can be operationalized across domains from autonomous vehicles to digital education agents.

This blog post functions as a primer and timestamped preview for those attending the session. It offers context on the paper’s contribution: reconciling high-level ethics with implementation-level design. The NRBC model is not just descriptive—it is procedural. It connects core human values with system functions through values-based audits, lifecycle alignment, and ethical resilience mechanisms.

The ICAD presentation will also feature the AI Moral Code’s visual architecture, address cross-sector validation, and open a discussion on integrating values like trust, transparency, and accountability into MLOps pipelines. Feedback from interdisciplinary attendees is essential to refine the model’s applicability and support its evolution as a global moral framework for AI systems.

Those interested can download the full paper and engage with ongoing development at aimoralcode.org.
